Director

Brian O’Connor

Director Statement

Measured in Thread is horror stripped to its essence: intimacy, control and violation.

No spectacle. No jump scares. Only the slow unraveling of safety into sterility. Comfort into captivity.

The story lives in the victim’s gaze as language meets needle. Poetry not as beauty but domination. Terror not shouted but whispered.

It’s a meditation on vulnerability, the weaponization of language and the haunting thin line between care and cruelty. Of how ideas once relegated to the fringes of society have become our new normal, even as they maim and demean us.

Bio

Brian grew up loving character driven films like All the President's Men and The Magnificent Seven. He writes stories with human stakes in which ordinary people do extraordinary things.

At the same time, he directs and produces short films that have been viewed over 500,000 times online and played at festivals worldwide.

He used to sell Cheerios.

Production Design

Bert & Trina Boutin

Bert Boutin is a Prop Master, Armorer & co-founder of Lemonade Acre. His work lives in the details—small, tactile elements that ground the story in reality. On Measured in Thread, he developed the practical components that interact directly with the actor, ensuring each movement feels precise, safe, and believable. The effect is subtle by design—what’s happening is real, even when it feels impossible.

Trina Boutin is a Production Designer & co-founder of Lemonade Acre. Her work is rooted in restraint, building environments that feel lived in, controlled and quietly unsettling. For Measured in Thread, she shaped a confined basement world where texture, light and negative space carry the tension as much as the performance. Every element is intentional, leaving just enough room for the audience to lean in.

Together, Bert & Trina blend design, props and practical effects into a single, controlled language. On Measured in Thread, their collaboration centers on restraint and precision, where every detail is considered and nothing is accidental. The result is a world that feels intimate, grounded, and quietly tense—built to hold the performance without ever distracting from it.
